# NOTES FOR DESIGNER / DEVELOPER

## Webflow Implementation Notes

1. **Hero sections** — Full-width, dark (#141414) background with white text. The electric lime (#C3E941) should be used for CTA buttons and key accent elements (stat numbers, hover states, active nav indicators). Not as body background.

2. **Trust strip** — Should feel weighty, not decorative. Consider a slightly lighter dark (#1a1a1a or #1e1e1e) background panel. Logos in white/light gray. Banking partner logos separate from client logos — they serve different credibility functions.

3. **Stat bar** — The "30+ Years | Billions Processed | 99.99% Uptime" section works best as a single-row grid with lime-accented numbers and white labels. Compact, not sprawling.

4. **Case studies** — These should feel like premium editorial content, not feature boxes. Consider a card layout with a prominent client logo, the key stat (99.99% uptime, 99.999% accuracy), and a short narrative. Link to full case study if expanding later.

5. **Solution pages** — Each should feel like its own landing page with industry-specific imagery. The hero should establish context (insurance = trust/speed, government = scale/compliance, ticketing = real-time/energy, university = modern/accessible).

6. **Security page** — This page needs to feel institutional and serious. Consider a structured grid of certification badges, then detailed sections below. Government and insurance buyers will spend time here — don't skimp on depth.

7. **Typography hierarchy** — Agressive for hero headlines and major section headers only. Lato for everything else. Don't overuse Agressive — its impact comes from restraint.

8. **Mobile** — Every page needs to work on mobile. The "How It Works" dual-flow (operations vs. recipients) should stack vertically on mobile, not side-by-side.

9. **CTA consistency** — Primary CTA is always "Request a Demo" (lime button). Secondary is "Talk to Sales" (outline/ghost button). Phone number (248.621.0900) should be visible on every page — enterprise buyers call.

10. **Social proof density** — Don't isolate proof in one section. Client names, stats, and trust signals should be distributed throughout every page. The goal is that a buyer can't scroll for more than 2 sections without encountering proof.

11. **Healthcare page** — This page needs to feel clinical-meets-enterprise. Consider a cooler color palette within the brand system (more white space, structured grid for the compliance badges). The HIPAA badge should be prominent — it's the primary differentiator for this audience. Healthcare buyers will go straight to the compliance section, so make it visually scannable.

12. **Transportation & Logistics page** — This page should feel operational and fast. Consider imagery that evokes movement — fleet, logistics, terminals. The EV shared usage payments angle is forward-looking and worth a visual callout (a card or badge) to signal innovation.

13. **Why NoCheck page** — This is the competitive positioning page. Design it as a confident editorial layout — not a feature comparison grid. Each differentiator should feel like a standalone proof point with visual weight. Consider a timeline or milestone visual for the 30+ years narrative. The "preferred service provider since 1996" line deserves its own callout moment.

14. **Prepaid cards** — Wherever prepaid cards appear as a payment method, consider a brief visual indicator or icon. For government and healthcare pages especially, the "reach unbanked populations" angle is a policy-relevant selling point worth emphasizing visually.

15. **RTP / FedNow** — These should be treated as premium payment rails throughout the site. Consider a small "instant" or lightning-bolt indicator wherever RTP and FedNow are mentioned. The Federal Reserve affiliation strengthens the FedNow connection — tie these together visually on the security page.
